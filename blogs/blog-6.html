Property Document Verification: 10 Must-Check Items

Legal checks are the foundation of a safe transaction. Use this short, practical checklist before committing funds.

  1. Clear title chain and ownership continuity.
  2. Sale deed and mother deed consistency.
  3. Encumbrance certificate with no hidden charge.
  4. Approved building or layout plan.
  5. Land-use and zoning compliance.
  6. RERA registration details where applicable.
  7. Property tax and utility dues status.
  8. No-objection certificates as required.
  9. Possession and payment schedule clarity.
  10. Agreement clauses for delay and exit terms.

Always seek professional legal review before signing final agreements. Early verification can prevent expensive disputes later.

Practical takeaway: Document discipline is the most cost-effective risk protection in real estate.
